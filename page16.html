HISTORY OF MAINIT

            There was once a couple who lived in Bila in the town of Bauko. One morning, their handsome dog was missing. Thinking that their dog might have just gone astray somewhere in the village, the couple searched every nook and cranny and asked everyone if they saw the dog. No one did! The couple even searched the nearby hills and dales but to no avail. Days passed and they gave up their dog for lost.

            Days later, as the couple was tending to the house the dog suddenly showed up at the door. The couple jumped for joy at the sight of their favorite pet. They hugged it but felt something was off in the dog’s fur. White crystals clung from all over its body. Upon closer inspection, they realized that it was salt. It really made them wonder where the dog had been. Despite that question being unanswered, they were happy that the dog came back. 

            Some weeks after, the man saw the dog leaving the village. He called for the dog but it only kept on walking. He got curious as to where the dog to so he just followed. Over the hill and mountain and even across streams he followed the dog. After walking for days, it stopped in a valley. The man approached it. It was wagging its tail and to the man’s amazement, he saw steam. As he got closer, he saw a stream of clear boiling water gushing from the ground. He sat to touch the water and then saw rocks covered with powdery white crystals. With his forefinger, he touched the crystals and sniffed and tasted it and lo it was salt! He smiled at the thought that his dog was smart enough to bring him to a wonderful place where there was boiling water and salt. Now he understood where the salt crystals on the dog’s fur came from.

            Excited, the man decided to bring his family to this place. He set out back for his village in Bila. His dog tagged along by his side. Upon reaching Bila, he ran to his hut to tell his wife of the wonderful discovery. After hearing her husband’s tale, the wife was equally excited. After that, they went out to tell the other villagers. A crowd gathered and listened to their tale of the place with boiling water and salt. The couple told the villagers of their plan to move to where their dog led them. Half of the villagers decided to go with the couple. At daybreak, after packing the belongings they needed, the couple and the rest of the villagers who decided to go with them set out to the place of hot springs and salt. The dog took the lead. After days of arduous travel, they reached the place that the man described. They called it Chongliyan and settled there. That place is Barangay Mainit today. 

            The first settlements in Mainit were built at Lengwa, the southern part of the present village. From Lengwa, the village expanded to include the northern part of the present Mainit. At Lengwa, the people built the first hot pool, diverting the streams of hot water to fill the pool. Even today, the people of Mainit still enjoy the healthy and relaxing mineral hot springs at daybreak before going to the fields and at sundown until early evening after working in the fields. The springs continue to draw people to Mainit up to this day.
