HISTORY OF MALIGCONG

            In the early days, the settlement of the people of Maligcong was at Fa-ang, the rice terraces of what is now the Maligcong Elementary School (The people of Maligcong today say that those who lived at Fa-ang came from a place called Kadangran). In Fa-ang, there was a couple who owned a pig that went missing. For many days, the couple searched in vain for the pig. One day, after an arduous search, they found the missing pig underneath a boulder near the entrance of what is now Barangay Maligcong. (The boulder which they speak of is presently located at the front yard of Mr. Timothy Mangatam’s residence). To their surprise, they found that the pig gave birth to several piglets. They realized then that the reason the pig went missing was to search for a more suitable environment in which to give birth as Fa-ang then was experiencing a heavy drought.

            Looking around the area where the pig gave birth, the couple saw that the place was more suitable for habitation as there was plentiful supply of clean water. They then decided to settle there. They went back to Fa-ang to gather their belongings and upon seeing them pack their things; several fellow villagers asked where they were off to. The couple answered, “We’re going to Maligcong and there we shall settle. We found our missing pig there and we saw that water is plenty.” 

            And so, the couple started the settlement in Barangay Maligcong. Other villagers from Fa-ang soon followed until no one was left in the old village. The old settlement was gradually converted into rice paddies. 

            Today, Maligcong has a land area of 1,089.49 hectares. Seven Hundred Seventy Seven Individual call this place their home.
