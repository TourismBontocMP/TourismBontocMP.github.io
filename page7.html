HISTORY OF ALAB PROPER

            When Alab was divided into Alab Oriente and Alab Proper in 1972. Alab Proper retained 213 hectares of land area. The sitios of Bengngasan, Bilig, Daligdig, Data and Mat-o, are sustained in this barangay.

            Having the same roots with the Alab Oriente population, the people of Alab Oriente descend from the family of the hunter from Samoki who started the first settlement across the river in Dongyowan (Alab Oriente). 

            With the passage of time, a certain person named Baclangen lost hi sow. He couldn’t find it anywhere in the village. Searching as far as he could, he crossed the river and climbed up the mountain. He found the pig in Bengngasan. It was so heavy to carry back to Dongyowan so he decided to leave it there. Every day, he brought food for the pig from Dongyowan. After some time, Baclangen realized that he could build his house near the pig instead. He transferred his family there and they became the first settlers of the place. 

            Later, the sow gave birth and a pen was constructed so that no pig would stray. At one time, a group of Spaniards passed by while the natives were enclosing the pen. They asked what the name of their place was. Thinking that they were being asked what they were doing, they answered, “Men-al-alad kami.”It was the word Alab that stuck to the Spaniards and so in their records, the place bore that name, which it carries up to the present time. 


(Tawid Celebrating 100 years of Bontoc Township Experience)
